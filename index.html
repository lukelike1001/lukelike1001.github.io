SmartQuake

A machine-learning model for long-term earthquake prediction

  • 4000 Years
  • 123 MB
  • 5147 Events
  • 4 Layers
  • 15 Observatories

Strong earthquakes are infrequent yet destructive events, mainly due to their unpredictability. Earthquake warning systems can only reactively send an alarm upon detection of an earthquake, which leaves only a small time frame for people to head to safety.
Led by Enmao Diao, The SmartQuake Project plans to utilize graph neural networks (GNNs) to predict both the location and time of an earthquake days in advance. More information can be found in the links below.

Privacy on Decentralized Social Networks

A research project measuring privacy and security concerns of Mastodon users
for handling data and personal information

  • Privacy and Security

    Are Mastodon users aware of how Mastodon handles their information? How do they feel about the privacy policy and online security on the platform?

  • Scraping and Surveying

    To measure privacy and security concerns from Mastodon users, we plan to post web surveys and utilize web scrapers on mastodon.social to collect data for our report.

  • Conference Paper

    We plan to publish our findings in a paper and look forward to submitting it to a conference by Fall 2023. Check back for more details!

This research project is organized by Dr. Pardis Emami-Naeini and is affiliated with the InSPIRe Lab at Duke University's Computer Science department.
